About LMIA

What's an LMIA?

An LMIA is a crucial step for employers in Canada to hire foreign workers. It demonstrates to the government that the employer has made a genuine effort to recruit a Canadian citizen or permanent resident but couldn't find a s uitable candidate. This ensures Canadians get priority in the job market, while also allowing employers to fill essential positions with skilled foreign workers like you.

Understanding the LMIA Process:

1. Employer applies for LMIA: The Canadian employer initiates the LMIA process for a specific job and location.
2. Government assessment: The government verifies that the employer made a good faith effort to recruit a Canadian candidate and assesses factors like offered wages and advertised qualifications.
3. Positive LMIA: If there's no qualified Canadian candidate and hiring a foreign worker won't negatively impact the job market, the LMIA is approved. This allows the employer to proceed with your work permit application.
4. Negative LMIA: If a Canadian candidate is available or there would be negative impacts, the LMIA is denied, and the employer cannot hire a foreign worker for that position.

LMIA Doesn't Guarantee a Work Permit:

While a positive LMIA is essential, the final decision on your work permit rests with Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).

Ready to Apply

If you find a suitable LMIA-approved job on Maple Match, you can proceed with your work permit application. The required documents typically include:

1. Completed application forms
2. Proof of identity (passport)
3. Proof of employment in Canada (job offer)
4. Additional documents may be required (proof of relationship, academic credentials, financial resources, medical exam results, etc.)

LMIA Exemptions:

In specific situations, employers might not require an LMIA to hire foreign workers. These exemptions often involve:

1. International agreements (NAFTA, CETA, etc.) for business visitors, intra-company transfers, or specialized professionals.
2. Ministerial exemptions for academics, researchers, or workers with rare skills.
3. Provincial/territorial agreements for specific occupations within a particular province.

Other Info

Note: LMIA requirements can vary depending on the job, location, and other factors. We recommend consulting the Canadian government website or an immigration professional for the latest information.

Currently, there are no known LMIA exemptions specifically for Nigerian workers. Canada's LMIA exemptions typically arise from broader international agreements, and Nigeria is not currently a participating country in these work permit-related agreements.
